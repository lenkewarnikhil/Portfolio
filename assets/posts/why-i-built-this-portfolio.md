# Why I Built This Portfolio (And What I Secretly Want From It)

I didn’t wake up one day and think, “Today I shall craft a perfectly structured, professional portfolio.”  
It was more like: *“My GitHub is chaos, LinkedIn feels like a resume graveyard, and I need a place that actually feels like **me**.”*

So this portfolio is my corner of the internet.  
Not just to show projects, but to record the messy parts of learning, the half-finished ideas, and the things I’m quietly proud of.

---

## The Real Reason: Proof For Myself

Everyone says, “Build projects. Build a portfolio.”  
But what they don’t say is how easy it is to forget how far you’ve come.

I built this site so that:

- I can **look back** and see how my writing, code, and thinking evolved.  
- I have a **home** for all the random experiments that don’t quite fit into a polished GitHub repo.  
- I can remind myself: *“Yes, I actually built all of this. It’s real.”*

This isn’t just for recruiters. It’s a quiet, ongoing conversation with my past and future self.

---

## What I Want This Space To Be

I want this portfolio to feel a bit like a handwritten notebook:

- **Unpolished thoughts** are allowed.  
- **Half-baked ideas** can still be shared.  
- **Wins and failures** both get a place here.

Some days it might be a deep-dive into AI or DSA.  
Other days it might just be me documenting a tiny CSS fix that took way too long.

Either way, it’s honest.

---

## If You’re Reading This

If you somehow found your way here:

- Maybe you’re a recruiter trying to see how I think.  
- Maybe you’re a fellow learner checking if you’re “behind” (you’re not).  
- Maybe you’re just curious.

Whoever you are, I hope this space feels human.  
Not just polished screenshots and buzzwords—but real thoughts from someone who’s still figuring things out.

---

## What’s Next

I’ll keep adding:

- New posts when I learn something worth sharing  
- Small project write-ups instead of just GitHub links  
- Honest reflections on what’s hard and what’s fun

If you stick around, you won’t see perfection.  
You’ll see **progress**—and that’s really what this portfolio is about.

Thanks for reading. 🙂
